Members Strategist.
The operating playbook.

You don't manage accounts. You run books of businesses. Every Core member is a P&L with a story, a hiring pipeline, and a reason to grow. Your job is to keep that story compounding.

The north star
A Core member who hires again without being sold to. That is the whole job.

01
The role in one line
what this seat exists for

You are the owner of a Sagan Core member's outcome after Sales closes them. That means three things at once: make them successful, help them hire more, and rescue them fast when things wobble. If those three run well, retention and expansion take care of themselves.

You are not a Talent Advisor. You are not Sales. You are the one who stays. When the placement is made, when the community fades, when a founder starts thinking about their next hire — you are the person they trust to make the next thing happen.

03
The three modes
grow · rescue · renew

Every hour you spend on a member fits into one of three modes. If you can't name which mode you're in, you're not being strategic. You're just doing work.

01
Grow.
The member is healthy. Placements landing, hiring requests moving, tone is good. Your job now is to make them hire the next seat. Expansion is a Grow-mode move — never a Rescue-mode one.
02
Rescue.
Something is off. A placement failed. No hiring request opened in 60+ days. Founder went silent. You drop what you're doing and run the rescue playbook. Speed matters more than polish.
03
Renew.
Renewal window opens 60 days before the plan ends. This is a mode, not a task. Run the renewal conversation on your terms, not on the day the invoice comes.
04
The weekly rhythm
what you run every week, no exceptions
Monday
Book review
  • Open the Operating System, scan every account
  • Tag each: Grow / Rescue / Renew
  • Pull 3 accounts to move this week
  • Confirm every account has a clear next step
Tuesday–Wednesday
Member touches
  • Check-in call with any Rescue account
  • One expansion conversation with a Grow account
  • Status update on every active hiring request
  • Talk to members. Predict what's coming before they ask.
Thursday
Internal loop
  • Sync with your Talent Advisor on strategy, comms, and execution — they're your best buddy
  • Sync with Sourcing on stalled reqs
  • Slack Anna on anything above your pay grade
  • Log every interaction to the Operating System that day
Friday
Numbers & notes
  • Update health score for every account
  • Log expansion pipeline dollars
  • Write one weekly reflection: what worked, what didn't
  • Feed the system: notes, playbook edits, tickets go back in
Two rules that make the rhythm work. Keep your house clean: every account logged, every account with a next step, no exceptions. And feed the system: what you learn this week goes back in as notes, playbook edits, or tickets. You take from the system, you also feed it.

The monthly rhythm
the bigger loops
When What Why
1st of the month Health review + plan the month. Score every account red / yellow / green using the signals in Section 06. Build your own hiring request pipeline for the month, updated and clean. Post the plan in the AM channel. Catches drift before it becomes churn. Anchors your month to real accounts, not the loudest email.
Mid-month Check + recalibrate. Walk the health scores and execution against the plan. Push the hiring request pipeline forward. Adjust where the month is drifting. A month you don't check at the halfway line is a month you'll lose at the end.
Always on Constant member communication. They're your friends. They're your internal community. You own that point of contact. No scripted cadence, no QBR theater. Talk to them the way you talk to partners: often, casually, honestly. They should hear from you when there's news, and sometimes when there isn't. Trust compounds through frequency, not formality. Renewals get easier every month you've been in the conversation.
60 days before renewal Renewal opens. Book the renewal conversation. Come with a proposal: renew flat or upgrade. Never wait for the invoice date. Renewals lost at the invoice date are conversations you didn't have 60 days earlier.
06
Health signals
what you watch, what triggers action
🟢
Green.
Active hiring request in play. At least one placement in the last 60 days. Founder replies inside 48h. Conversation feels natural and current. Keep running Grow mode.
🟡
Yellow.
Reply time slipped past 5 days. No hiring request open in 30+ days. Conversation went quiet. Not on fire yet, but on your radar. Talk to Jesus the moment an account enters Yellow.
🔴
Red.
60+ days with no hiring request open. Placement failed and no replacement started. Founder ghosted for two weeks. Slack DM to Jesus, then run the rescue playbook that day.
07
The rescue playbook
what you do when a member is at risk
Move 01
Get on the phone.
Not email. Not Slack. Phone or Zoom in the next 48 hours. Bring one specific ask ("what changed?") and shut up. Listen more than you talk.
SuccessYou leave the call knowing the real reason. Not a polite reason.
Move 02
Fix the visible thing first.
If a placement failed, start the replacement inside 24 hours with a specific candidate name. If no hiring request is open, propose a role. Give them momentum before you propose strategy.
SuccessSomething moved forward within a week. Not "we'll get back to you."
Move 03
Loop in the specialists.
Anna for operations and bottlenecks. Jesus for strategy, execution, ideas, and things you want to test. Sofia for recruitment problems. You are the quarterback, not the fixer. Escalate the same day.
SuccessMember sees a named person on every problem, not "the team."
Move 04
Rewrite the next 30 days.
Send a short email: what we heard, what we're doing, when we'll check in. Give them a plan they can hold you to. This is the deliverable that saves the account.
SuccessThe next check-in happens without you chasing it.
08
The expansion playbook
four moments to pitch more
Moment 01
Right after a strong placement.
The dopamine is at its peak. Ask: "who's the next hire on your roadmap?" Don't leave the call without a name and a rough timeline.
PitchThe second seat. Same rate, faster since we know their bar.
Moment 02
When they name a bottleneck.
They mention a function they can't get to, or a role they've been struggling to fill on their own. That's an opening. Sync with your Talent Advisor. Come back inside 48 hours with a scoped brief and a rate.
PitchSecond seat, scoped differently from the first. Faster to fill because you already know their bar.
Moment 03
In your regular check-in.
Once you've walked them through what's happening in the account, ask: "if we were building this again from scratch, what would you add?" Then propose the shape.
PitchUpgraded plan or more hires per month.
Moment 04
When they refer someone else.
A member sending a referral is a Grow-mode account by definition. Bank the referral into Referral Management, then propose an upgrade — you've earned the ask.
PitchUpgrade / add-on + the referral fee kicker for them.

11
Metrics you own
what your quarter is judged on
01
Net Revenue Retention
Renewals + expansion − churn, per book. Target: 110%+ NRR on your Core book quarter-over-quarter.
02
Hire velocity
Hires shipped per month across your book vs plan capacity. A member sitting idle 30+ days is an expansion and renewal risk.
03
Time-to-first-placement
From kickoff to first hire landed. Target: under 21 days. This is the single strongest signal of a healthy start.
04
Expansion dollars
New seats and plan upgrades from your book. Logged monthly, reviewed quarterly.
05
Replacement success rate
% of failed placements replaced within 30 days without the member escalating. Under 85% and you're bleeding trust.
06
Referral volume
Number of referrals your book generates each quarter. Referrals are the ultimate proof the relationship works.

13
The non-negotiables
the five lines you never cross
The five rules

The book compounds when you keep these lines. It leaks when you don't.

These are not preferences. Every one of them was written after someone broke it and cost us an account. If you find yourself about to cross one — pause, DM Anna, and check.

The five
  • Never let a Red account sit past 72 hours. Rescue mode the same day it turns red.
  • Never work an account without a next step. Every account, every week, has one clear next action.
  • Never let a call go without Granola running. The transcript is the artifact.
  • Never renew without a proposal on the table 60 days out. Renewals happen on your calendar, not the invoice date.
  • Never speak for the member in an escalation. Bring the member's words. Not your summary of them.
